# Antiflow

> **Go against the flow of vibecoding.**

Vibe coding is making developers lazy. Coding isn't supposed to be like watching youtube, it's supposed to be like building a masterpiece, brick by brick. Antiflow does the boring stuff, refuses to write all the logic, and hands you the tasks that matter. It protects your skills from rotting. Take the wheel back.

## What is Antiflow?
Antiflow is an AI coding skill/protocol. Instead of generating your entire application, Antiflow acts as your AI Tech Lead. It scaffolds out your boilerplate code, configures the environment, and then intentionally leaves **strict tasks** for you to complete based on your role and seniority level.

## How it works

1. **Intake**: You provide your `ROLE` (e.g. Backend, Frontend, Mobile), your `LEVEL` (Junior, Mid, Senior), and your `AI_ASSISTANCE` preference.
2. **Scaffolding**: Antiflow builds the project skeleton, configs, and boilerplate.
3. **Stubs**: Wherever domain logic belongs to *you*, Antiflow leaves a `// TODO: [USER]` stub.
4. **TODO.md**: Antiflow generates a centralized `TODO.md` file for you, which acts as your ticket queue.
5. **Refusal Protocol**: If you ask the AI to complete your assigned tasks, it will politely refuse and tell you to get to work.

## Installation & Usage

To use Antiflow with your AI agent:
1. Clone this repository or copy the `SKILL.md` file into your AI's context window.
2. Tell your AI: "Activate the Antiflow protocol."
3. Follow the AI's prompt to provide your parameters:
   - `ROLE`: e.g., `Backend`, `Frontend`, `Fullstack`, `Mobile`, `Security`
   - `LEVEL`: `junior`, `mid`, `senior`
   - `PROJECT`: What you're building
   - `STACK`: Your preferred language/framework (or "no preference")
   - `AI_ASSISTANCE`: `0%` (strict), `50%` (shared), or `100%` (AI does all)

Get ready to write some code!
